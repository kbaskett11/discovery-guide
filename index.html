Stage 1 · Situation
Build context. Understand the landscape.
Open the conversation. Get them talking about their world — team, tools, process, goals — before you pitch anything.
💡 Rep tip: Ask "What does good look like for your team this year?" — lets them set the agenda and reveals the strategic priorities that actually matter.
Company size & structure
"Tell me a bit about your team — how big is marketing, how's it structured?"
Current customer marketing process
"Walk me through how you currently gather and use customer proof."
Top business priorities this year
"What are the 2–3 things your team is most focused on in the next 12 months?"
What's driving this conversation?
"What made you take this meeting today — what changed?"
Auto-saving
Stage 2 · Problems & Pain
Dig into what's broken.
Don't present solutions yet. Understand the pain deeply — the gap between where they are and where they want to be.
💡 Rep tip: Use "Tell me more about that" 3 times before you move on. Surface-level pain = weak deal. Deep pain = real urgency.
Primary pain / problem
"What's the biggest challenge you're running into with customer proof today?"
Secondary pain
"What else is frustrating about the current process?"
What have they tried?
"Have you tried to solve this before? What happened?"
Cost of doing nothing
"What happens if this doesn't get solved in the next 6 months?"
Pain ownership — who feels this most?
"Who else on your team deals with this problem day-to-day?"
Auto-saving
Stage 3 · Impact & Vision
Quantify the gap. Paint the picture.
Help them articulate what winning looks like — and what it's worth to get there. This is your ROI foundation.
💡 Rep tip: If they can't quantify impact, ask "How does this problem show up in a number that matters to your CMO/CRO?" — force it to a metric.
Business impact of the problem
"How is this showing up in your numbers — pipeline, win rate, content volume, churn?"
What does success look like?
"If this was completely solved a year from now, what would be different?"
Must-have requirements
"Are there any non-negotiables — things a solution absolutely must do or have?"
Nice-to-haves
"What would make this even more valuable — a wish list?"
Technical requirements
"From an integration standpoint — what must this connect to? Salesforce, Marketo, anything else?"
Security & compliance
"Does your security team have a vendor review process? Any compliance certifications required?"
Auto-saving
Stage 4 · Decision Process
Map the path to a signed contract.
Understand how decisions get made, who's involved, and what could slow this down. Don't leave this stage without a clear map.
💡 Rep tip: Ask "What would have to be true for you to sign by [date]?" — makes the timeline concrete and surfaces hidden blockers early.
Decision timeline
"When are you looking to have something in place?"
Budget status
"Is budget allocated, or does this need to go through an approval?"
Budget range
"What's the ballpark you're working with for something like this?"
Who signs the contract?
"Who's the ultimate decision maker — who writes the check?"
Decision criteria
"How will you evaluate vendors — what does the winning solution need to nail?"
Potential blockers
"What could get in the way of moving forward — what's your biggest concern?"
Agreed next steps
Before you end the call — confirm the next step with a specific date.

Stage 5 · Stakeholders
Map the buying committee.
Identify every team involved in the decision. Note who you've met, who you still need access to, and what each team cares about.
💡 Rep tip: "Besides yourself, who else will weigh in on this decision?" — keep asking until you've got all 9 teams accounted for or explicitly ruled out.
